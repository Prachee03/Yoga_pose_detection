Importance of Yoga


Physical Health: Yoga promotes physical strength, flexibility, and balance, leading to better posture and reduced risk of injuries.


Mental Well-being: Regular practice of yoga reduces stress, anxiety, and depression by calming the mind and promoting relaxation.


Emotional Balance: Regular yoga practice increases self-awareness, fostering a deeper understanding of oneself and others.


Boosted Immunity: Yoga stimulates the immune system, promoting overall health and resilience against illnesses.


Improved Sleep Quality: Yoga relaxes the body and mind, leading to better sleep quality and patterns.


Lifestyle Alignment: Yoga encourages a holistic approach to life, promoting healthy habits, mindful eating, and positive relationships.


Community and Support: Yoga communities offer social support, camaraderie, and a sense of belonging, contributing to overall well-being.

Types of Yoga Poses

Baddha Konasana Pose


Description

Begin seated on the floor with your legs extended. Bend your knees and bring the soles of your feet together, allowing your knees to fall outward. Hold onto your feet or ankles with your hands. Sit up tall with your spine straight. Gently press your knees toward the floor while keeping your spine lengthened.


Benefits

Stretches the inner thighs and groins

Opens the hips

Relieves mild depression and anxiety

Eases menstrual discomfort


Try It

Bitilasana Pose


Description

Start on your hands and knees in a tabletop position, with your wrists directly under your shoulders and your knees under your hips. Inhale as you arch your back, lifting your chest and tailbone towards the ceiling. Your belly will move towards the floor. Lift your head slightly, gazing forward or slightly upward.


Benefits

Stretches the Front Body

Improves Posture

Relieves Stress


Try It

Padmasana Pose


Description

Start by sitting on the floor with your legs extended and your spine erect. Bend your right knee and bring your right foot to the top of your left thigh, with the sole of your right foot facing upward and your heel close to your abdomen. Bend your left knee and bring your left foot to the top of your right thigh, with the sole of your left foot facing upward and your heel close to your abdomen. Rest your hands on your knees in a comfortable position, with your palms facing upward and your fingers in a mudra if desired.


Benefits

Stimulates Digestion

Calms the Mind

Increases Concentration
